The New Model

The traditional way of starting a seniors cohousing project is that a group of successful professionals who are close to retirement and have high financial and organizational resources buy land and build a cluster of houses close to each other. Every individual owns their place but all share common living areas. While this option works very well for individuals who have the resources to execute such a project, it is not feasible for the majority of seniors. Aisha Real Estate Development Inc. aims to develop cohousing communities that offer rental units to seniors. This way they can enjoy the benefits of cohousing without the need to go through the process of establishing the project itself or the financial burden of buying a unit in such a project.

The New Model

A cohousing community of one hundred individuals would be optimal. A community of one hundred seniors is large enough for seniors to socialize but not too large to know each other and bond together. What Aisha Real Estate Development Inc. aims to do is to establish a development which is an aggregation of these cohousing communities. So if we have an aggregation of 10 cohousing communities we will have a larger community of 1000 seniors. Regardless of the number of seniors in each cohousing community and the number of cohousing communities within the aggregation, the idea is to develop a structure where each cohousing community acts as a nucleus within this structure.

The New Model

Figure 1 shows a hypothetical model of such an aggregation in case of the number of cohousing communities is four and each community consists of five apartment buildings. The value of the proposed aggregation is that while the dynamic of the small cohousing community is preserved within each community, the bigger structure enables us to provide a set of services tailored specifically toward the seniors population. It is more economical and affordable to provide a specialized set of services to a collection of cohousing communities rather than to a single community.

The New Model

For instance there could be a common building that serves all cohousing communities in the project where all services needed by seniors could be found like: Medical center where geriatricians are the primary care doctors Gym with training programs developed specifically for seniors Restaurants and cafes. Entertainment center .A place for large social gatherings and seminars. Transportation service •Grocery store •More services could always be added when needed A guest house where seniors children and other family members could stay when they visit the seniors will be a great addition to the project.

The New Model

Understanding the needs of the seniors we target will allow them to age-in-place and use the platform I am suggesting to its full potential. So, while the majority of seniors do not need special care services, we could establish a facility dedicated to seniors who would need special care. The purpose of this facility is that if a senior needs change after joining the community, they would not need to look for another type of accommodation. It would be cruel to ask a senior who has lived in the community and established bonds with other seniors to leave when they are most vulnerable!

The New Economy

As Alberta is associated in our minds with oil I would like Ontario to be associated in the Canadians minds as the perfect place to spend their retirement. There are 6.5 million seniors in Canada and I want to convince one million of them to move to Ontario! As a province, as developers and businesses we need to rally around building as many as possible of the new communities I am suggesting and put Ontario on the national map as the dream land for seniors. When one million seniors call Ontario home, that means their family members will come to visit, say once a year, which means we will have few million tourists per year. Just imagine the businesses that will start around those tourists! This is just one aspect of the economy that will flourish and lead other aspects of the economy to flourish as well.

The New Economy

However this is not true! When seniors come to Ontario, they will need many services. These services, the large scale construction that will take place, and the flourishing of tourism will create many jobs which will encourage young Canadians and immigrants to also come to Ontario.
